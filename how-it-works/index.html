Security

Elegant Authentication

Login credentials built into the email address itself

The Problem with Email Auth

Websites have login forms. APIs have auth headers. But email? No built-in authentication. Other services force awkward workarounds—special headers users can't set, body prefixes that clutter messages, or trusting easily-spoofed From addresses.

Our Solution: Token Addresses

Your address 7086-3612@motd.com contains your credentials. The first part (7086) is your permanent user ID—like a username. The second part (3612) is your rotatable PIN—like a password. Authentication is invisible; you just send to an address.

Two-Factor by Design

Your token only works when sent from your registered email. Even if someone sees your address, they can't use it without also controlling your email account. Two factors, zero friction.

Rotate Your PIN

Change your PIN anytime—your user ID stays the same. During the grace period, both old and new PINs work so nothing breaks. Like changing a password, but simpler.

Works Everywhere

Any email client, any device, any script that can send mail. No special configuration, no SDK, no headers to set. If you can send email, you can authenticate.

Zero Retention

We process your message and send the response. Nothing stored. Your email client is your archive—nothing on our servers to hack, subpoena, or breach.
